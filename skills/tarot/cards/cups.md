# Cups

Full card meanings for interpretation. Load this file after drawing Cups cards.

**Element:** Water
**Domain:** Emotion/Relationships

## Ace of Cups

**Themes:** New emotional beginning, opening of the heart, love offered freely, spiritual and emotional awakening, the wellspring of feeling
**Situations:** Falling in love or beginning a meaningful relationship, feeling your heart open after a closed period, experiencing creative inspiration with emotional depth, receiving or offering compassion, starting therapy or emotional healing work
**Shadows:** Emotional overflow without boundaries, love that demands return, sentimentality without substance, drowning in feeling, refusing the offered cup
**Symbols:** Overflowing cup (abundance of emotion), hand from cloud (divine gift), dove descending (spiritual love and peace), lotus blossoms (spiritual opening), five streams (senses awakened by feeling)

---

## Two of Cups

**Themes:** Partnership, mutual attraction, emotional connection, balanced exchange, recognition of the other, harmonious union
**Situations:** Meeting someone who feels like a mirror, forming a business partnership based on shared values, reconciling after conflict through honest exchange, recognizing a soul connection, committing to mutual support
**Shadows:** Codependency, losing yourself in relationship, forcing connection where none exists, superficial harmony avoiding real issues, need for external validation through partnership
**Symbols:** Two figures facing each other (mutual recognition), caduceus of Hermes (healing through connection), lion head (courage in vulnerability), wreaths (celebration of union), equal cups (balanced exchange)

---

## Three of Cups

**Themes:** Celebration, friendship, community, shared joy, collaboration, creative abundance through connection
**Situations:** Celebrating with close friends after an achievement, participating in a creative collaboration that flows easily, attending a wedding or reunion, finding your people, experiencing the joy of being truly seen by others
**Shadows:** Superficial socializing, avoiding solitude, competitive friendship, celebration as distraction from problems, group dynamics that exclude, hangover from excess
**Symbols:** Three women dancing (joyful collaboration), raised cups in toast (shared celebration), autumn fruits (harvest of connection), flowing garments (ease and movement), garlands (festivity and adornment)

---

## Four of Cups

**Themes:** Contemplation, emotional apathy, meditation on what is versus what could be, dissatisfaction, withdrawal to reassess
**Situations:** Feeling unmoved by options that once excited you, withdrawing from social life to process feelings, being offered something but unable to appreciate it, questioning whether current relationships fulfill you, sitting with discontent rather than rushing to fix it
**Shadows:** Ingratitude, self-absorption, missing opportunities through inaction, depression masked as contemplation, refusing gifts out of pride or fear
**Symbols:** Figure under tree (withdrawal from engagement), three cups before him (known options), hand from cloud offering fourth cup (unseen possibility), crossed arms (closed off), stable meditation posture (stillness that could be stuckness)

---

## Five of Cups

**Themes:** Loss, grief, regret, focusing on what's gone rather than what remains, emotional disappointment, mourning
**Situations:** Processing the end of a significant relationship, dwelling on a professional failure while missing current opportunities, grieving what you hoped would happen, feeling regret over words you can't take back, standing at the site of loss before you can move forward
**Shadows:** Wallowing, refusing comfort, addiction to grief, ignoring what remains in focus on what's lost, bitterness, victim mentality
**Symbols:** Three spilled cups (loss), two upright cups behind (what remains), cloaked figure (hidden from what's still available), bridge to home (path forward exists), distant castle (return possible but not yet taken)

---

## Six of Cups

**Themes:** Nostalgia, childhood memories, innocence, past connections resurfacing, sweetness of what was, gifts from the past
**Situations:** Reconnecting with an old friend over shared memories, returning to your hometown and feeling flooded with the past, encountering something that reminds you of childhood joy, receiving unexpected kindness that feels familiar, healing through revisiting earlier times
**Shadows:** Living in the past, idealizing what was, regression to childish patterns, refusing to grow up, using nostalgia as escape from present
**Symbols:** Children exchanging flowers (innocent giving), cups filled with flowers (sweet memories), medieval setting (the past), younger child offering (gifts from earlier self), older child receiving (present self accepting past)

---

## Seven of Cups

**Themes:** Fantasy, illusion, difficult choices, imagination run wild, seeing possibilities but lacking clarity, enchantment and confusion
**Situations:** Feeling overwhelmed by options and unable to commit, fantasizing about different life paths instead of choosing one, seeing your wishes laid out but unsure which is real, being seduced by appealing illusions, creative brainstorming before the editing phase
**Shadows:** Escapism, addiction to fantasy, avoidance through endless option-generation, analysis paralysis, self-deception, chasing mirages
**Symbols:** Seven cups floating in clouds (ungrounded options), figure in silhouette (unclear vision), snake (temptation or danger), dragon (fear or challenge), castle (fantasy of achievement), jewels (material desire), laurel wreath (glory), veiled figure (mystery or delusion)

---

## Eight of Cups

**Themes:** Walking away, emotional departure, seeking something more, leaving behind what no longer fulfills, the quest for deeper meaning
**Situations:** Leaving a job that pays well but drains your soul, ending a relationship that's comfortable but not truly intimate, moving away from your hometown to find yourself, abandoning a project you've outgrown, beginning the journey toward emotional truth
**Shadows:** Running away from commitment, grass-is-greener syndrome, abandoning what could be healed, fear of depth disguised as seeking, leaving before the real work begins
**Symbols:** Figure walking away (departure), eight cups stacked (what's being left), gap in cups (something missing), mountain ahead (challenging journey), night sky (moving into the unknown), staff (support for journey), water's edge (emotional threshold)

---

## Nine of Cups

**Themes:** Satisfaction, emotional fulfillment, contentment, wishes granted, pleasure in what is, the satisfied heart
**Situations:** Feeling genuinely content with your life circumstances, celebrating personal achievement without need for external validation, experiencing a period of emotional well-being, enjoying the fruits of emotional work, having what you truly wanted
**Shadows:** Smugness, complacency, selfish satisfaction, physical pleasure without emotional depth, surface contentment avoiding deeper needs, the temporary high of getting what you wanted
**Symbols:** Figure sitting contentedly (satisfaction), nine cups arranged like wishes (fulfillment), crossed arms (self-assurance), cloth behind (abundance), feast table suggested (material comfort supporting emotional contentment)

---

## Ten of Cups

**Themes:** Emotional completion, family harmony, lasting happiness, love made manifest in lasting form, community and belonging, the heart's home
**Situations:** Experiencing your family or chosen family in deep harmony, creating a home that feels emotionally safe for everyone, reaching a place of peace after long emotional work, celebrating an anniversary or family milestone, witnessing love that has matured into lasting commitment
**Shadows:** Idealization of family, pressure to appear happy, sacrificing individual needs for group harmony, using family as excuse not to grow, fear of threatening "perfect" situation
**Symbols:** Rainbow (promise fulfilled), couple with arms raised (celebration and gratitude), dancing children (innocent joy), comfortable home (emotional stability made real), cups arranged in rainbow arc (complete emotional spectrum), green land (fertile ground for love)

---

## Page of Cups

**Themes:** Emotional beginner, sensitive messenger, creative dreaming, youthful openness to feeling, curiosity about the heart, gentle approach to new emotional territory
**Situations:** Receiving news that touches your heart, beginning to explore your creative gifts, approaching someone with vulnerable honesty, learning about your emotions through therapy or journaling, being surprised by depth of feeling
**Shadows:** Emotional immaturity, oversensitivity, escapist fantasy, dramatic feelings without follow-through, naivety about emotional complexity, getting lost in daydreams
**Symbols:** Youth holding cup (beginning relationship with feeling), fish emerging from cup (message from the unconscious), ornate clothing (taking feeling seriously), curious expression (wonder and openness), calm sea (emotional waters not yet deep)

---

## Knight of Cups

**Themes:** Romantic pursuit, following the heart, emotional quest, idealistic love, the lover and artist in motion, graceful approach
**Situations:** Pursuing someone with romantic gestures, following creative inspiration wherever it leads, making decisions based on what feels right rather than what makes sense, offering your heart openly, traveling for emotional or artistic reasons
**Shadows:** Moodiness, melodrama, unreliable romanticism, seduction without substance, following feeling without discernment, the "love bomber"
**Symbols:** Knight on horse (romantic quester), winged helmet (elevated imagination), cup held forward (offering heart), slow-moving horse (dreamlike pace), flowing stream (following emotional current), decorative armor (beauty over practicality)

---

## Queen of Cups

**Themes:** Emotional mastery, compassionate presence, intuitive depth, nurturing through understanding, the counselor and empath, feeling wisdom, creating emotional safety for others
**Situations:** Holding space for someone's difficult feelings without trying to fix them, trusting your intuition about someone's unspoken needs, offering comfort that goes to the heart of pain, balancing deep empathy with clear boundaries, being the person others trust with their secrets
**Shadows:** Emotional manipulation, martyrdom, absorbing others' feelings as your own, using empathy as control, drowning in others' needs, hiding truth to keep peace
**Symbols:** Ornate throne by water (mastery of emotional realm), enclosed cup (contained rather than spilled feeling), sea creatures (comfort with emotional depths), angels on throne (divine compassion), calm presence (unshaken by feeling's intensity)

---

## King of Cups

**Themes:** Emotional wisdom, balanced feeling, compassionate authority, mastery without suppression, diplomatic skill, the counselor and healer who's done their own work
**Situations:** Leading others through emotional crisis with steady presence, balancing empathy with clear judgment, maintaining emotional equilibrium during chaos, being trusted to mediate because you honor all sides, offering wisdom earned through feeling
**Shadows:** Emotional manipulation, using wisdom as power, suppressing feeling behind calm facade, demanding emotional labor from others, cold compassion, the "rational" approach that dismisses feeling
**Symbols:** Throne on turbulent water (stability amid emotional intensity), cup in one hand and scepter in other (feeling balanced with authority), fish amulet (emotional wisdom), ship in background (navigating emotional waters), royal bearing (mastered presence), calm amid waves (inner steadiness)
